The Big Question

If 75–85% of your R&D workflows could be reshaped by AI agents this year — what's your excuse for not having a plan?

That figure isn't a futurist projection. It's from McKinsey's 2025 Cognitive Generative Agent Readiness report, and it's already being acted on. The question for R&D leaders isn't whether agentic AI will change their labs — it's whether they'll be the architect of that change, or its casualty.

From R&D to R&P — The Agentic Shift Is Here

We've spent two years debating generative AI. That debate is over. 2026 is the year the C-suite is demanding a shift from AI experimentation to AI performance — agentic systems that reason, plan, execute multi-step workflows, and learn from outcomes.

Self-driving labs are the clearest signal. Closed-loop environments where AI formulates hypotheses, dispatches robotic systems to run experiments, analyzes results in real time, and iterates — with minimal human intervention. Nature listed it as one of the top technologies to watch. By early 2026, platforms like ORGANA and Strateos are already commercially operational.

The bottleneck isn't the technology — it's maturity readiness. Organizations that bolt agentic AI onto broken processes will fail. Organizations with clean data infrastructure and cross-functional workflows will transform.

The IDC 2026 Manufacturing FutureScape: by 2028, 65% of G1000 manufacturers will use AI agents with design and simulation tools to continuously validate product configurations. That's not a pilot — that's a structural shift in how NPD pipelines operate.

Three questions for your next leadership review:

  • 01Do we have the data governance to feed agentic systems clean inputs?
  • 02Are our development pipelines modular enough to insert AI agents without rebuilding everything?
  • 03Are we building AI orchestration skills, or still hiring for prompt engineering?

Yann LeCun's AMI Labs raises $1.03B for World Models

LeCun left Meta and closed a $1.03B seed round ($3.5B pre-money) — Europe's largest seed round on record. AMI Labs builds on JEPA: AI that predicts in abstract representation space, not token by token. Applications target robotics, process control, and healthcare.

→ LLMs can't understand physical causality. World models might — and this is the architecture that will eventually run your factory floor autonomously.
