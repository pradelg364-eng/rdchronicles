01 The Big Idea

From R&D to R&P — The Agentic Shift Is Here

We've spent two years debating generative AI. That debate is over.

2026 is the year the C-suite is demanding a shift from AI experimentation to AI performance — and in R&D, that means agentic systems: autonomous agents that don't just generate text, but reason, plan, execute multi-step workflows, and learn from outcomes.

Self-driving labs are the clearest signal. These are closed-loop R&D environments where AI formulates hypotheses, dispatches robotic systems to run experiments, analyzes results in real time, and iterates — with minimal human intervention. Nature listed it as one of the top technologies to watch in 2025. By early 2026, platforms like ORGANA and Strateos are already commercially operational.

"The bottleneck isn't the technology — it's maturity readiness. Level 1–2 organizations will bolt agentic AI onto broken processes. Level 4–5 organizations will transform with it."

— RD Chronicles, Edition #1

The IDC 2026 Manufacturing FutureScape puts a hard deadline on this: by 2028, 65% of G1000 manufacturers will use AI agents with design and simulation tools to continuously validate product configurations. That's not a pilot — that's a structural shift in how NPD pipelines operate.

Three questions every R&D VP should be asking now:

  • 01 Do we have the data governance to feed agentic systems clean inputs?
  • 02 Are our development pipelines modular enough to insert AI agents without rebuilding everything?
  • 03 Are we building our team's AI orchestration skills, or still hiring for prompt engineering?

Signal 01

Siemens Digital Twin Composer + NVIDIA Omniverse — industrial metaverse goes live

Launched at CES 2026, Digital Twin Composer combines Siemens Xcelerator PLM with NVIDIA's physically accurate simulation. PepsiCo deployment: 20% throughput gain, 10–15% Capex reduction, ~100% pre-build validation. AI agents run what-if scenarios before any physical change.

Why it matters: The digital twin is no longer a dashboard — it's the engineering decision layer.
